What is your company going to make? Please describe your product and what it does or will do.
We build realistic virtual environments to train and evaluate AI agents. Users plug in their agents and workflows; our engine simulates behavior, finds failure points, and generates behavioral data. We then use that data to fine-tune agents using reinforcement learning or alpha-evolve-style techniques. Like autonomous vehicles trained on billions of simulated miles, we bring that same philosophy to enterprise agents in sales, operations, and support and ultimately all other agents.

Why did you pick this idea to work on? Do you have domain expertise in this area? How do you know people need what you're making?
We’ve seen firsthand that AI agents often fail not because of weak models, but because they’re never trained or tested in realistic environments. Alexandre led the creation of Meditron, the top open-source medical LLM. Guillaume built agent evaluation pipelines at BCG X across multiple industries. This helped us realize: no one is seriously productizing simulation infrastructure for agent training. That’s the gap we’re going after.
Who are your competitors? What do you understand about your business that they don't?
Our competitors include internal simulation teams at AI labs (like DeepMind, OpenAI), academic platforms like BabyAGI/Gymnasium, and early startups focused on agent benchmarking or fine-tuning. What they miss is that environments, not just models, are the core infrastructure bottleneck for agents. Labs build internal tools but never share them (they will have difficulty diversifying their in their environments); open-source platforms are too shallow for real use; and startups often focus on agents themselves, not the data and feedback loops that make agents work. We’re building the infrastructure layer that unlocks realistic simulation at scale, with a modular system for domain-specific environments, something no one has productized in a general, reusable way.
How do or will you make money? How much could you make?
Phase 1: we work with AI labs and startups to build custom simulation environments for their agents, solving real problems up close and refining reusable infrastructure. Phase 2: we evolve into a self-serve platform where users can create, simulate, and improve agents using modular components. Long-term: • Labs training agents via online RL will need behavioral data at scale, similar to how they once relied on Scale AI. • Enterprises building internal agents will need tools to test and fine-tune them. With strong execution, this can become a $10M+ ARR business serving both segments.
